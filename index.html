Aribeth Mitchell is an ordinary student at Cambridge University, or so she thought. From the charming old villages nestled in the Japanese Alps, to the mystical Egyptian Desert, Aribeth will have to deal with a world wrapped up in greed, wrath, lust and hypocrisy, where love is stained in blood and lies, and where evil also might have a beating heart. Mighty fights, mythical and mystical creatures and entwining loves await her in an adventure that will change her life forever.

Mythology · Locations · Lore

The Black Dragon

Kuragari — God of Chaos. Raven horns, obsidian wings, one ice-blue eye and one crimson. Born in Izumo — or so he claims.

The White Dragon

Takemikazuchi — God of Thunder & Storms. Sent to watch Kuragari. White horns, wide wings, golden eyes that see everything.

The Ancients' Temple

Deep within the Japanese Alps, three synchronised entities of unparalleled power await. Both Shinya and Ken know them. Aribeth will soon discover their interest in her is anything but coincidental.

Hinote — 火の手

Hand of Fire. The first true villain. Charming in Kyoto, catastrophic thereafter. His alter-ego Apophis is the eternal darkness that Ra must defeat at every dawn.

Apophis / Apopi

The ancient Egyptian embodiment of darkness, evil and chaos — eternal enemy of Ra and Maat, the great serpent that must be slain at every dawn.

Ra & the Dawn War

Ra, God of the Sun, must kill the serpent Apophis every single day in order to let the sun rise. A task requiring the aid of Seth — God of storms, deserts and disorder — whose role expands dramatically in the second book.

Maat — Truth & Order

Goddess of truth, justice and cosmic balance. The eternal opposite of Apophis. Her principles underpin the moral war at the heart of the series — the question of whether evil can also have a beating heart.

Seth

God of storms, deserts and disorder — Ra's great ally against Apophis. His relationship to Takemikazuchi grows more complex as the series crosses into Greek mythology in Book Two.
